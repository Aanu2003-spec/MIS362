Exercise04-Critical Thinking With AI

Data Integrity

Research "Who is Wazoo at Winona State University?" in three different generative AI models of your choice.
Reflect on the results between the models and consider:

  • Are the answers consistent?
  • Are the answers accurate?
  • Can you prove or disprove any of the statements?

(50) 3. Reflect on this research process. How do you know whether you can trust the results?


Precise Calculations

Prompt Bank-Study, Exams, Research

Copy any of these into Copilot or ChatGPT and replace the angled brackets with your topic/course.

Study Support (concepts & readings)

You are my study coach for <CourseName>.
Summarize this concept at a first-year level: <topic>.
Give: 1) a 150-word summary, 2) a 5-bullet checklist of key ideas,
3) two common misconceptions, 4) a 3-question self-quiz (answers hidden).

Exam Prep (active recall)

Act as a quiz generator for <topic>.
Create 10 mixed-difficulty questions across recall, application, and analysis.
After I answer, grade concisely and explain the reasoning for each item.
Then suggest 3 targeted review items based on my mistakes.

Research & Sources (credible work)

I am researching <research question>.
Propose 3 search strategies with keywords and Boolean operators.
List 5 credible sources with a 1-2 sentence rationale for each.
Draft a 150-word abstract outline with 3 APA citation placeholders.

Homework & Problem Solving (show your work)

Help me solve this problem step-by-step: <problem>.
Ask clarifying questions first.
Show a worked solution with labeled steps and a final check.
Add 2 similar practice problems (no solutions).

Project Planner (scaffolded output)

You are my project planner for <assignment> in <CourseName>.
Produce: 1) a one-paragraph aim, 2) a 5-step plan with time estimates,
3) risks and mitigations, 4) a checklist I can paste into my notes.

Source Checking (verification)

Given this claim: '<claim>', provide: 
1) specific search queries to verify it, 
2) likely authoritative sources, 
3) what evidence would confirm or refute it, 
4) a short paragraph on remaining uncertainty.

Concept Compare/Contrast

Compare and contrast <concept A> vs <concept B>.
Provide: definition, key differences (table), use-cases, and a quick rule-of-thumb.

Code/Formula Explainer (Excel, Python, SQL, etc.)

Explain what this code does, step-by-step, add comments, then show a simpler version:
<paste code or formula>
Include 2 mistakes beginners often make and how to avoid them.

Ethics & Limitations

For <topic/task>, list: 1) ethical risks, 2) data/privacy concerns,
3) ways to mitigate them, 4) what should be cited when I use AI in my assignments.

Next Steps

With Generative AI being so prominent in our lives, you may want to consider signing up for the University of Helsinki Course, ' Elements of AI '.

The goal of this course is to demystify AI

The elements of AI is a free online course for everyone interested in learning what AI is, what is possible (and not possible) with AI, and how it affects our lives  - with no complicated math or programming required. By completing the course you can earn a LinkedIn certificate.  The course is available from May 14, 2018.

After taking the course, you will be able to:

  • Understand some of the major implications of AI
  • Think critically about AI news and claims 
  • Define and discuss what AI is
  • Explain the methods that make AI possible
